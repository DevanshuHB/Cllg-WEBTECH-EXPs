What is Ethical Hacking?

Posted in Blogs | CyberSec Diaries

Ethical hacking is the practice of legally and responsibly testing computer systems, networks, and applications to identify security vulnerabilities. Ethical hackers help organizations improve their security by finding weaknesses before malicious attackers exploit them.

Who is an Ethical Hacker?

An ethical hacker is a cybersecurity professional who uses hacking techniques with proper authorization to improve system security. Ethical hackers follow strict rules and work within legal boundaries.

Types of Hackers

  • White Hat Hackers: Authorized security professionals.
  • Black Hat Hackers: Malicious attackers.
  • Grey Hat Hackers: Hackers who fall between white and black hats.

Why Ethical Hacking is Important

  • Identifies system vulnerabilities
  • Prevents cyber attacks
  • Improves overall security posture
  • Ensures compliance with security standards
