What is Cybersecurity?

Posted in Blogs | CyberSec Diaries

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting computers, networks, servers, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, or damage. With the increasing use of the internet and digital systems, cybersecurity has become an essential part of modern life.

Cyber attacks can target individuals, organizations, and even governments. These attacks may aim to steal data, disrupt services, or gain unauthorized control over systems.

Why is Cybersecurity Important?

Cybersecurity is important because almost everything today depends on digital systems. From online banking to social media, sensitive information is stored electronically.

  • Protects personal and sensitive data
  • Prevents financial losses
  • Ensures privacy and trust
  • Protects organizations from cyber threats

Common Types of Cyber Threats

There are many types of cyber threats, but some of the most common ones include:

  • Malware: Malicious software designed to damage systems.
  • Phishing: Fake emails or websites that steal user data.
  • Ransomware: Attacks that lock data and demand payment.
  • Password Attacks: Guessing or cracking weak passwords.
